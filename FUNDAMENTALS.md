# The Fundamental Layer

Everything we build sits on top of this. If a venture doesn't trace back to one of these primitives, it doesn't belong on the list.

Six load-bearing ideas. Read top to bottom.

---

## 1 · Why Trade Exists

Two parties value the same thing differently. The overlap between what A will accept and what B will pay is the **Zone of Possible Agreement (ZOPA)**. Every business that has ever existed lives inside this zone.

- **Seller's floor** — won't sell below this number
- **Buyer's ceiling** — won't pay above this number
- **ZOPA** — the band between them, where both walk away better off
- **Price discovery** = finding any point in this band

No ZOPA = no trade. The width of the ZOPA = the size of the prize.

---

## 2 · The 5 Primitives That Create ZOPAs

A ZOPA doesn't appear by magic. It exists because the two parties differ along at least one of these five axes. These are the only primitives. Every opportunity reduces to one or more.

1. **Spatial** — access to different markets / resources / geographies
2. **Temporal** — need now vs. can wait (the entire lending industry)
3. **Information** — who knows what about prices, supply, demand
4. **Preference** — same thing valued differently by different people
5. **Capability** — some can do things others can't

**Test:** Name a business. Trace the value it captures to one of these five. If you can't, you're describing a tool, not an arbitrage.

---

## 3 · Why Most Gaps Stayed Unbridged (Until Now)

A gap is only economic to bridge when the **value of bridging it** exceeds the **cost of bridging it**. Humans have a high cost floor. That floor cut off millions of real gaps.

Two axes:
- **X — Gap size** (value at stake)
- **Y — Cost to bridge**

Three zones, top to bottom:
- **Above the human floor** — too expensive even for humans. Real gaps that never got bridged because the work itself cost more than the prize.
- **Between human floor and agent floor — "Newly economic"** — millions of gaps that were uneconomic at human cost but profitable at agent cost. This is the entire opportunity.
- **Below the agent floor (~0)** — basically free to attempt.

Examples:
- $14/mo subscription renegotiation → tiny gap, way below human floor, fits inside agent zone
- Conference room shopping across chains → medium gap, same logic
- Enterprise M&A → large gap, already economic above the human floor (not the interesting zone)

**The only thing that changed:** agents collapsed per-transaction cost toward zero. Everything else is consequence.

## 4 · One Activity, Four Vocabularies

These look like different industries. They are not. They are the same loop dressed for different audiences.

All four mean: **find the number where both win.**

- **Trade** — economics vocab
- **Price discovery** — markets vocab
- **Negotiation** — commercial vocab
- **Arbitrage** — trader vocab

If you're building in one of these spaces and your competitors are framing in a different vocab, you're often the only one who sees the full market.

---

## 5 · The Build Fork — Tool vs. Loop Owner

Once a gap becomes economic, you can build two very different things. Same opportunity, different companies, different physics.

### Sell the tool
"Here's software, you operate it."
- SaaS pricing
- Customer owns the outcome
- Lower revenue per account
- Faster to ship
- Commodity risk over time

### Run the loop
"We own the outcome, pay us on results."
- % of value captured
- We own the metric
- Higher revenue per account
- Slower to ship, harder to copy
- Defensible if we hit ~50% loop coverage

**Sweet spot:** loops that are ~50% automatable today with an obvious trajectory. 80% automatable = tool in disguise. 20% = too early.

---

## 6 · The Decision Funnel — From Idea to "Yes, Build It"

Every opportunity passes through this funnel. If it fails a layer, it's killed or parked — not moved forward "because it's interesting."

1. **L1 · Is there a real ZOPA?** Which of the 5 primitives creates the gap?
2. **L2 · Is the gap newly economic?** Was it uneconomic at human cost, economic at agent cost?
3. **L3 · Can we credibly own the outcome?** Tool vs. Loop — which side are we on?
4. **L4 · Is the loop ~50% automatable now?** Not too early, not already a commodity.
5. **BUILD.**

## The One-Line Test

Before adding anything to the dashboard, answer in one sentence:

> "This captures value because **[party A]** and **[party B]** differ along **[primitive 1–5]**, the gap was uneconomic at human cost but economic at agent cost, and we own the **[tool / loop]** side."

If the sentence doesn't complete cleanly, it's not ready.
