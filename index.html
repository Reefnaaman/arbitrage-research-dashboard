Traditional: Human sees opportunity → Human acts

AI Model: AI sees opportunity → AI acts immediately

Value: Speed + Network Scale + Pattern Recognition

▶ 4 Types of AI Arbitrage

1. Information Velocity: Act faster than humans process

2. Network Scale: Contact 1000+ vs human's ~10

3. Pattern Recognition: Spot opportunities humans miss

4. Attention Scale: Monitor everything simultaneously

Pure Information Arbitrage

▶ 7 Sources of Information Asymmetry

1. Geographic: Local info → Global networks

2. Platform: Info on Platform A → Buyers on Platform B

3. Language/Cultural: Language A → Language B networks

4. Time: Time A info → Time B buyer availability

5. Expertise: Technical info → Business applications

6. Scale: Small info sources → Large buyer networks

7. Formality: Informal info → Formal buying processes

Validation Framework

Phase 1: Market Reality Check

Do people actually negotiate these things?
Is there real money on the table?
Are current solutions inadequate?

Phase 2: Technical Feasibility

Can we access the data needed for intelligent negotiation?
How standardized are the negotiation processes?
What legal/compliance constraints exist?

Phase 3: Business Model Viability

How do we reach decision-makers?
Customer acquisition cost vs. lifetime value?
What defensible advantages can we build?

Phase 4: Scaling & Network Effects

Does more usage create better outcomes?
Can we bring both buyers and sellers onto platform?
Winner-take-most dynamics potential?
