Agentic Product Criteria

▶ What Makes a Good Agent Opportunity?

Information Dense: Agent can process data humans can't handle at scale

Network Effects: More usage = better outcomes for everyone

Standardizable: Patterns the agent can learn and improve on

High-Frequency: Enough transactions to train and optimize

Clear Value Capture: Percentage of savings, not subscription fees

▶ Red Flags to Avoid

Too Much Physical Complexity: Shipping containers, warehouse logistics

Already Solved Markets: Where arbitrage doesn't actually exist

Regulatory Nightmares: Financial services, healthcare compliance

One-Time Transactions: No repeat business, no network effects

Crowded Competition: SaaS cost reduction with many players

Strategic Focus Areas

▶ Digital-First Industries

Why Digital: Lower friction, faster iteration, easier to scale

Supply/Demand Inefficiencies: Where information doesn't flow efficiently

Examples: Platform pricing, liquidation discovery, talent matching

Avoid: Physical goods with shipping/logistics complexity

The 5 Fundamental Difference Types

▶ Core Framework: What Creates All Arbitrage Opportunities

1. Spatial Differences → Some have access to resources/markets others don't

2. Temporal Differences → Some need money now, others can wait (entire lending industry)

3. Information Differences → Some know market prices/opportunities others don't

4. Preference Differences → Same thing valued differently by different people

5. Capability Differences → Some can do things others can't


Key Insight: AI negotiation platforms capture value by reducing information differences while exploiting the other four types of differences.

Business Application: Look for markets with high information asymmetry + remaining spatial/temporal/preference/capability gaps that still create negotiable value.

Validation Framework

Phase 1: Market Reality Check

Do people actually negotiate these things?
Is there real money on the table?
Are current solutions inadequate?

Phase 2: Technical Feasibility

Can we access the data needed for intelligent negotiation?
How standardized are the negotiation processes?
What legal/compliance constraints exist?

Phase 3: Business Model Viability

How do we reach decision-makers?
Customer acquisition cost vs. lifetime value?
What defensible advantages can we build?

Phase 4: Scaling & Network Effects

Does more usage create better outcomes?
Can we bring both buyers and sellers onto platform?
Winner-take-most dynamics potential?
